Join our first round of Design, Technology, & Innovation Fellows

The role

As a fellow, you’ll serve as a senior practitioner on multidisciplinary project teams with career civil servants, actively demonstrating the value of iterative development and user-centered design. Our teams will work to improve a wide range of city services, which may include researching and redesigning our permitting process, building the next generation of ATXFloods, improving the digital presence of our Convention Center, standing up open-source solutions for the Open Government Partnership, or creating city-wide standards for UI design, cloud hosting, and automated testing.

You’ll also serve as an expert and educator in your discipline, establishing new practices that can work across departments and providing guidance and training for other fellows and city staff.

Qualifications

The position includes the following preferred qualifications:

  • At least three years of professional experience working on large-scale digital experiences, interfaces, or applications
  • Ability to make strong technical contributions in at least one of the following disciplines:
    • User Experience Design
    • Visual Design
    • Content Strategy
    • User Research
    • Process & Workflow Design
    • Front-end Development
    • Back-end Development
    • Data Analytics
    • Project Management
    • Product Management
    • Technology Policy
  • Knowledge of lean and/or agile design and development methodologies, including expertise in determining when and how to use specific approaches, deliverables, and facilitation methods
  • Demonstrated ability to share knowledge through establishing standards, mentoring team members, leading training sessions, and writing for the public

Pay and benefits

Between $32.47 and $41.34 an hour, commensurate with experience. Fellows will serve a one-year term working up to 40 hours each week.

Fellows hired in 2016 will serve in temporary positions, which means that they will not be eligible for paid time off or health insurance. Fellows who work beyond one year would become eligible for health insurance through the city.

Once hired, all fellow temporary employees are considered at will employees. Because they are temporary positions, they are not covered by the Municipal Civil Service rules of the City of Austin.

Timeline

The first round of fellows will start on September 6th for a 1-year, extendable term. Some fellows may be able to start in July or August based on their skillsets and the needs of our projects. We will be reviewing applicants and offering positions on a rolling basis, so apply soon!

Application requirements

In your application, we'll ask you to identify your primary discipline areas from the list above and submit 2-3 work samples that exhibit your abilities in those areas. With your work samples, we want you to describe the goals of the project, how you reached your solutions, and your role on the team.

You can attach your work samples in the "Optional Documents" section of the application or include a URL where we can access them. Work samples are required for us to consider your application.

We also have two short essay questions (around 250 words each):

  1. Why do you want to work as a Design, Technology, and Innovation Fellow for the City of Austin?
  2. Tell us about a time when you used design and/or technology to help improve a system, service, or organization

What we'll look for in our reviews

Analysis and creative problem solving: Expertise determining user needs or identifying market opportunities and designing and deploying innovative solutions

Tools and execution: Experience in iterative design, development, and deployment of solutions using current and emergent technologies and best practices

Empathy and emotional intelligence: Experience in client-facing roles, navigating processes and bureaucracy, and delivering services with a strong user-facing or consumer-oriented element

Ability to work well on teams: Experience collaborating with folks from other disciplines on a daily basis, appreciating differences in perspective while also providing deep expertise in their area of specialization. (We’re a large organization, so we want to see evidence that you can thrive in a large organization.)
