Director of Performance & Innovation

Description

As the Director of Performance & Innovation, you’ll build and lead the Office of Performance & Innovation at the California Department of Justice. In this capacity, you'll serve as the master evangelist and silo-breaker at the California Department of Justice, tasked with building relationships across the California Department of Justice, sourcing talent and work, and supporting the performance and innovation team in their daily efforts.

In your role, you will collaborate with the performance and innovation team and career civil servants on multidisciplinary projects, actively demonstrating the value of iterative development and user-cetnered design. Our teams will work to improve a wide range of department services, which may include redesigning our citizen help center, improving the background check process, and collaborating with department heads on determining key performance indicators and building dashboard to aid departments in managing progress towards goals.

You’ll also serve as an expert and educator in your discipline, establishing new practices that can work across departments and providing guidance and training for the performance and innovationt eam and department staff.


Qualifications

The position includes the following preferred qualifications:

  • Experience in a management position, with proven success bureaucracy-hacking in government and/or a large organization
  • Enthusiasm about the power of technology, knowledgeable about its benefits, and a network to bring in the right technology and design talent
  • Outstanding capability to win hearts and minds, including high emotional intelligence and exceptional communication and storytelling skills
  • Action-oriented to push projects forward, but patient and persistent enough to see them through despite frustrations and roadblocks
  • Knowledge of lean and/or agile design and development methodologies, including expertise in determining when and how to use specific approaches, deliverables, and facilitation methods
  • Demonstrated ability to share knowledge through establishing standards, mentoring team members, leading training sessions, and writing for the public

Pay & Benefits

Between $77,436 and $111,324 per year, commensurate with experience. This position is eligible for paid time off and health insurance through the state.


Location

Sacramento/San Francisco


Timeline

Application Deadline: Monday, August 29, 2016

We will be reviewing applicants and offering positions on a rolling basis, so apply soon!


Application Requirements

Resume: 1-2 page resume

Statement of Interest: In your application, we'll ask you to submit a statement of interest (also known as a "Statement of Qualifications"). The Statement of Interest should discuss your professional and educational background and experience and how your skills meet the minimum qualifications and desirable qualifications of this position. In addition, please feel free to include why you are interested in this position and how you hope to contribute to the Office of Performance & Innovation.


What we'll look for in our reviews

Analysis and creative problem solving: Expertise determining user needs or identifying market opportunities and designing and deploying innovative solutions

Tools and execution: Experience in iterative design, development, and deployment of solutions using current and emergent technologies and best practices

Empathy and emotional intelligence: Experience in client-facing roles, navigating processes and bureaucracy, and delivering services with a strong user-facing or consumer-oriented element

Ability to work well on teams: Experience collaborating with folks from other disciplines on a daily basis, appreciating differences in perspective while also providing deep expertise in their area of specialization. (We’re a large organization, so we want to see evidence that you can thrive in a large organization.)
