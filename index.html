About Us

OPII is a new unit inside the Executive Office of the California Department of Justice. We are a small, mission-oriented team that — working closely with DOJ leadership — helps identify the organization’s biggest needs, tackle its toughest problems, and support organizational modernization and performance transformation.

Mission

Our mission is to help empower the CA DOJ to deliver a more effective, efficient, and equitable justice system and improve constituent services for all Californians through the deployment of 21st century approaches and tools.

What we do

  • ENTERPRISE EVALUATION. Identify Department-wide opportunities and risks, conduct and coordinate program assessments, and recommend areas of actionable and achievable improvements.
  • PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT. Facilitate strategic planning, provide visibility on departmental priorities, and promote the use of metrics to inform decision-making and organizational effectiveness.
  • CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT. Deploy tools, training, workshops, consulting, and implementation support to improve business processes, operations, and client services in a digital era.
  • INITIATIVE IMPLMENTATION. Pilot, coordinate, launch, and stand-up new and/or cross-cutting initiatives, products and services in partnership with management and staff.

Example Projects

Types of projects you'll work on:

  • Transform digital services by building and applying modern technologies to make services more accessible, efficient, and effective.
  • Improve criminal justice system policy, transparency and accountability by improving data collection, analysis and reporting.
  • Improve the way the agency hears from the public and handles consumer complaints to make enforcement more responsive and data-driven.
  • Redesign the agency website ‘front-door’ to make services easier to find and more-user friendly.
  • Redesign the criminal history background check system to improve speed, accuracy and customer-experience and to better meet the needs of job-seekers, employers, and the criminal justice system.
  • Apply technology, data and user-insights in innovative ways to enhance the DOJ’s reach and impact.
  • Improve performance across the agency by developing performance metrics, applying data to operational challenges, and using data to aid service improvement and decision-making.

Our Approach


User first

Understanding the challenges and needs of users is key to building better services. We work closely with our customers—within DOJ and Californian constituents—to create solutions that better meet their needs by employing a user-centered design philosophy.

Data driven

Data is an enterprise asset and a powerful driver of change. We use it to generate new insights, make informed decisions, provide transparency, and leverage our resources more wisely.

Digital forward

Technology offers the ability to drastically increase impact and effectiveness. We develop digital services that make transactions easier to accomplish and help lead the transition to a tech-powered organization.

Delivery focused

Policy and implementation must go hand-in-hand. We work with management and their staff to ship on-time and on-budget—setting concrete goals, developing project plans and milestones, and undertaking iterative and supportive processes to deliver results.
