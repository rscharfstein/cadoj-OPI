Reforming Criminal Background Checks




The California Department of Justice processes more than two million background checks a year. Most of these are for the purposes of securing employment, housing, or education, while other are from individuals seeking to clean up their record. The process can be very slow, relies entirely on paper forms and snail mail, and provides information which is difficult to read and sometimes incomplete or inaccurate. These challenges create barriers for those with criminal records to secure the opportunities they need to play a productive role in our society. The Office of Performance & Innovation is collaborating with staff across the department to reform this process end-to-end by exploring user needs and designing solutions. These solutions include moving the user experience online, automating internal workflows, improving the accuracy of back-end systems, and redesigning the way information is presented to the reader.
