Director of Performance & Innovation

Do you want to build a better government by leveraging technology, data, and design for internal agents and citizens? Are you motivated by owning high-impact products and services? Does being an advocate for your team and inspiring them to achieve their best motivate you? Are you an agile superhero/superheroine who wants to lead the charge on government 2.0? Then the California Department of Justice is looking for you.

Under the general direction of the Chief Deputy Attorney General, the Director of the Office of Performance and Innovation leads the Attorney General's team devoting to identifying departmental issues and problems, providing an active monitoring and management role, making policy recommendations for implementation, and installing new services to meet the core needs of the Attorney General’s Office and the California Department of Justice. This position represents the Attorney General before other state, federal, and local agencies with regard to audits, transformational change, and improvement projects conducted by external organizations.

You’ll also serve as an expert and educator in your discipline, establishing new practices that can work across units and providing guidance and training for the Office of Performance & Innovation as well as agency staff.

Qualifications

We are looking for someone with the following skills:

Knowledge of...

  • Principles, practices, and trends of public administration, organization, and management
  • Techniques of organizing and motivating groups
  • Program evaluation and development
  • Methods of administrative problem solving
  • Developments in civic technology, user-centered design, principles and practices of policy formulation and development
  • Personnel management techniques

Ability to...

  • Plan, organize, and direct the work of multidisciplinary professional and administrative staff
  • Analyze administrative policies, organization, procedures and practices
  • Integrate the activities of a diverse program to attain common goals
  • Gain the confidence and support of top level administrators and advise them on a wide range of operational and administrative matters
  • Develop cooperative working relationships with representatives of all levels of government, the public, and the Legislative and Executive Branches
  • Analyze complex problems and recommend effective courses of action
  • Prepare and review recommendations and reports
  • Support the Attorney General and Division staff in the implementation of recommendations and priorities

In addition, this position includes the following preferred qualifications:

  • Experience displaying proven leadership ability in driving change in large organization(s), coaching teams towards positive culture, and acting as a dependable liaison across units.
  • Demonstrated passion for civic innovation/technology and a belief in the mission of making government better.
  • Experience executing and delivering on complex, cross-agency projects requiring the overcoming of bureaucracy, internal, and external barriers.
  • Experience leveraging data, technology, and user-centered design in organizations to create better, more efficient external and internal services.
  • Experience with auditing and performance management best practices, proven continuous improvement successes, and program evaluation techniques.
  • Experience with developing and implementing policy to adapt to operational changes and procedures.

Pay & Benefits

Commensurate w/ experience, up to $111,324 per year. This position is eligible for paid time off and health insurance through the state.

Location

Sacramento, CA and San Francisco, CA

To apply, please submit the following documents:
