Chief Performance & Innovation Officer


Under the general direction of the Associate Attorney General, the Chief Performance & Innovation Officer -- also know as, Director of the Office of Performance Improvement and Innovation (OPII) -- directs staff to identify departmental opportunities and problems and to make policy and operational recommendations and supports implementation with the various and diverse programs of the Attorney General’s Office and the California Department of Justice (DOJ). This position represents the Attorney General before other state, federal, and local agencies with regard to audits, transformational change, and improvement projects conducted by external organizations.

Minimum Qualifications

Knowledge of...

  • Principles, practices, and trends of public administration, organization, and management
  • Techniques of organizing and motivating groups; program evaluation and development/li>
  • Methods of administrative problem solving
  • Developments in civic technology, user-centered design, principles and practices of policy formulation and development; personnel management techniques
  • The organization and functions of California State Government including the organization and practices of the Legislature and the Executive Branch
  • The department’s or agency’s equal employment opportunity objectives; and a manager’s role in the equal opportunity program

Ability to...

  • Plan, organize, and direct the work of multidisciplinary professional and administrative staff
  • Analyze administrative policies, organization, procedures and practices
  • Integrate the activities of a diverse program to attain common goals
  • Gain the confidence and support of top level administrators and advise them on a wide range of administrative matters
  • Develop cooperative working relationships with representatives of all levels of government, the public, and the Legislative and Executive Branches
  • Analyze complex problems and recommend effective courses of action
  • Prepare and review recommendations and reports
  • Effectively contribute to the department’s or agency’ equal employment opportunity objectives
  • Support the Attorney General and Department staff in the implementation of recommendations and priorities

Desirable Qualifications

  • Experience executing and delivering on large, complex projects and leading teams that delivered results.
  • Experience in driving transformational change in government and/or large organizations, completing projects with multiple stakeholders, building internal and external support, and overcoming barriers.
  • Strong communication and interpersonal skills with a demonstrated ability to oversee the work and performance of administrative and technical staff.
  • Passion about, and demonstrated experience in, leveraging data, technology, and user-centered design in organizations to create better, more efficient external and internal services.
  • Experience with performance management best practices, development of strategic plans, reviews/audits, continuous improvement successes, and/or program evaluation techniques.
  • Demonstrated ability to identify opportunities and risks/problems, evaluate and prioritize alternative solutions, and implement selected approaches.

Civil Service Title

Career Executive Assignment (C.E.A)

Civil Service Examination

For the Career Executive Assignment (C.E.A) position, there is no additional examination beyond the application requirements. For this position, Standard State Application and the Statement of Qualifications is considered the "examination" and is scored by a panel of subject matter experts. The panel separates candidates into 6 ranks based on their scores, and only candidates in the top 3 ranks can be hired. You'll receive notice of your score, after which we will start extending interviews.


Pro tip: Be sure to address the minimum and preferred qualifications in your Statement of Qualifications!

Background Check & Special Requirements

The position requires the incumbent pass a Background Investigation prior to being hired. The Background Investigation may include criminal background, fingerprinting, and a review of any governmental records.

Pay & Benefits

Commensurate w/ experience, up to $111,324 per year. This position is eligible for paid time off and health insurance through the state.

Location

Sacramento, CA and San Francisco, CA

Timeline

Application Deadline: November 15, 2016

We will be reviewing applicants and offering positions on a rolling basis, so apply soon!

Application Requirements

To apply, please submit the following documents:

  • Standard State Application: Complete this form and submit with your application.
  • Statement of Qualifications: Please submit a Statement of Qualifications that discusses how your education, training, experience, and skills meet the minimum qualifications and desirable qualifications and qualify you for the position. The Statement of Qualifications demonstrates your ability to present information clearly and concisely in writing and should be typed and no more than two pages in length.
  • Resume: 1-2 page resume
