Open Positions in Office of Performance Improvement & Innovation

The Office of Performance Improvement & Innovation is a new unit at the California Department of Justice DOJ, with a mission to help empower the CA DOJ to deliver a more effective, efficient, and equitable justice system and improve constituent services for all Californians through the deployment of 21st century approaches and tools.


The Chief Performance & Innovation Officer will be the Director of this new office, and will lead the effort to build out performance improvement, innovation, and transformational change capacities at DOJ. In this position, you’ll be charged with setting the priorities of the new office, building a team and attracting talent, developing service offerings to meet core agency needs, and advancing the mission of the office and agency as the leader of this new start-up team. You will collaborate closely with agency leadership, leading efforts to improve service quality, and developing, evangelizing and delivering 21st century tools to solve agency challenges.


Under the general direction of the Chief Deputy and Associate Attorney General, the Chief Performance & Innovation Officer will lead the Attorney General's team devoted to identifying departmental opportunities and problems, providing an active performance management role, making policy recommendations for implementation, and installing new services to meet the core needs of the California Department of Justice and constituents. This position represents the Attorney General before other state, federal, and local agencies with regard to audits and improvement projects conducted by external organizations You’ll also serve as an expert and educator in your discipline, establishing new practices that can work across units and providing guidance and training for the Office of Performance Improvement & Innovation as well as agency staff.


This is a career civil service position that will serve the agency and Attorney General, regardless of the administration. Click here to learn more

The California Justice Information Services Division (CJIS) serves as the main IT provider for the Department of Justice, and helps design, deliver and maintain technology and data solutions that power the DOJ, law enforcement across the state, and the criminal justice system.


The CA DOJ is in the process of modernizing our capabilities, and making data and technological changes in order to better meet the needs of users, enhance our analytical capacities, and aid criminal justice system improvements. As part of this effort, we are looking for an experienced Senior Database Architect who will design and build a modern and scalable system to better collect, audit, store, and analyze complex, multi-source data sets critical to criminal justice system functioning. The ideal candidate will lead a matrix team of functional IT/ database admins in the definition of policies, standard, procedures and applications necessary to aggregate, clean and analyze data produced by different groups.


The Senior Database Architect is responsible for the management and control of a variety of electronic data analysis, reporting, and processing services which are provided to clients throughout the Department of Justice to assist them in meeting their program objectives. These integral services include the analysis of user needs, system design, development of technical specifications, personal computer and mainframe software selection, software development and maintenance, testing, problem resolution, development of user guides, monitoring the performance of operational systems, and user consulting. The Senior Database Architect provides leadership and direction to ensure effective and efficient maintenance of existing systems and development of new highly complex, statewide systems.


The Senior Database Architect is a civil service position in the California Justice Information System’s Integrated Applications and Services Section and has the classification of “Data Process Manager III" in the state system.
