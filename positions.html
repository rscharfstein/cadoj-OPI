Open Positions in Office of Performance Improvement & Innovation

Chief Performance & Innovation Officer / Director of OPII

Under the general direction of the Chief Deputy Attorney General, the Chief Performance & Innovation Officer leads the Attorney General's team devoted to identifying departmental issues and problems, providing an active monitoring and management role, making policy recommendations for implementation, and installing new services to meet the core needs of the Attorney General’s Office and the California Department of Justice. This position represents the Attorney General before other state, federal, and local agencies with regard to audits, transformational change, and improvement projects conducted by external organizations.


You’ll also serve as an expert and educator in your discipline, establishing new practices that can work across units and providing guidance and training for the Office of Performance Improvement & Innovation as well as agency staff.

Open Positions in the California Justice Information Services Division

We’re also upping our digital and data game with new hires across the agency. You may be interested in the following roles in the Department of Justice’s California Justice Information Services (CJIS) Division.


CJIS serves as the main IT provider for the Department of Justice, and helps design, deliver and maintain technology and data solutions that power the DOJ, law enforcement across the state, and the criminal justice system.


Operations Manager, Digital Delivery Unit (DDU)

Under the general direction of the CJIS Division Director, the Operations Manager will be responsible for functioning as a highly-skilled, independent consultant for the newly formed Digital Delivery Unit (DDU). DDU is charged with the implementation of powerful solutions to the major challenges in criminal justice in California.


As an inaugural member of this unit, this position will be required to establish and develop procedures and workflows that will impact the entire Department of Justice (DOJ). The Operations Manager will manage and maintain strong relationships with a broad set of stakeholders; consult with internal and external experts to generate innovative solutions and assess their feasibility; develop clear and appropriate implementation plans; monitor CJIS Division projects that cross Bureaus and impact other DOJ divisions; and keep the CJIS Division Director and all relevant stakeholders apprised of each project’s status.

Web Team Manager

The Web Team Manager supervises a team of highly skilled developers, analysts, and consultant resources in support of the Department's Web content, applications, and digital services. The incumbent performs oversight and provides guidance at the highest technical level on large and highly sophisticated DOJ systems, modules, routines, and interfaces. In addition, the Web Team Manager will provide technical services to the user community in application development, software/hardware evaluation, procurement document development, planning, and coordination. An in-depth knowledge of the Department's data processing environment and the ability to lead and mentor technical staff is essential.


The Web Team Manager will also be responsible for ensuring the work efforts of Development Support Unit are planned, organized, coordinated, and managed to achieve optimum end results. The Web Team Manager will collaborate with end users, stakeholders, engineers, designers, contractors, legal teams, and procurement personnel to build consensus and deliver modern and constituent-centric products with good user interface/user experience. The Web Team Manager will develop an understanding of the Department’s project management methodology based on the Project Management Institute’s Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMBOK) and an in-depth understanding of information technology and the system development life cycle (SDLC).

Database Architect

The Database Architect in the Integrated Applications and Services Section (IASS) is responsible for the management and control of a variety of electronic data analysis, reporting, and processing services which are provided to clients throughout the Department of Justice to assist them in meeting their program objectives. The Database Architect is a highly skilled technology manager with experience directing legislative analysis, knowledge of strategic and tactical planning; knowledge of Federal and State IT policies; knowledge of cost benefit analysis; ability to plan and oversee subordinate supervisors; ability to plan, schedule and oversee project tasks, dependencies and deliverables, and to prioritize and implement activities in accordance with the department’s strategic IT plan.


The Database Architect assists with the bureau’s overall management, budget and organizational objectives. The services provided include the analysis of user needs, system design, development of technical specifications, personal computer and mainframe software selection, software development and maintenance, testing, problem resolution, development of user guides, monitoring the performance of operational systems, and user consulting. The Database Architect provides leadership and direction to ensure effective and efficient maintenance of existing systems and development of new highly complex, statewide systems including new systems resulting from legislative mandates. The Database Architect must also have the ability to oversee the work activities and deliverables of consulting personnel in accordance with contract services agreements.
